About This Day

India celebrates Independence Day every year on 15th August to mark the nation's freedom from British rule in 1947. This day is a reminder of the sacrifices made by our freedom fighters who fought bravely for our independence.

Our first Prime Minister, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, hoisted the national flag at the Red Fort in Delhi on this day. Since then, it has been a tradition to hoist the flag and sing the national anthem across the country.

Independence Day is a symbol of pride, unity, and the spirit of India. It reminds us of our rich cultural heritage, diversity, and the responsibilities we have as citizens of this great nation.
